About

Kyle Boon

I'm married to my wife Ann and have two daughters. We live in Saint Paul, Minnesota. In addition to being a husband, father and software engineer, I am a woodworker, a cook, a bread baker, a daily chess player, and an avid reader of fantasy and science fiction novels (check out my favorite books). I sometimes post about these on Instagram.

I build things slowly in the shop. So far I've made a dining table, several frames, and a nice workbench. Right now I'm working on some small boxes — photos from the shop. I got back into chess during the pandemic and try to solve puzzles and play at least one game a day.

I'm a Principal Software Engineer at RB Global. I've been building backend systems for about twenty years, mostly in the JVM ecosystem, and I've spent the last several years focused on distributed systems and cloud architecture. I like the puzzle of fitting systems together and the number of options it gives you in designing solutions. I really enjoy figuring out how the business and tech come together in a domain model. You can find my complete professional background on LinkedIn or view my detailed resume.

I spend a lot of my time at work mentoring other engineers, especially people who show interest in programming beyond just a job. I like discussing the why of something, not just the how. I've also given several technical presentations over the years on JVM development, API design, and going framework-free.

I write here occasionally about backend development, craft, and whatever I'm tinkering with.

I earned a Bachelor of Engineering in Computer Science and Engineering from The Ohio State University and have a healthy interest in the Buckeyes.
